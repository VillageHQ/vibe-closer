# Email Templates

Use these as starting points. **Always personalize** -- swap in specific details from their company, products, recent news, or your mutual connection.

---

## Warm Intro Request to Mutual Connection

**Subject**: Intro request -- {{hiring_manager_name}} at {{company_name}}

**Body**:
```
Hi {{mutual_connection_name}},

I'm exploring Head of Product roles in dev tools and {{company_name}} is high
on my list. Their work on [specific product/feature] aligns really well with
my background building API platforms and developer experience at Notion.

Would you be comfortable introducing me to {{hiring_manager_name}}? I'd love
to learn more about what they're building. Happy to send you a blurb to
forward if that's easier.

Thanks,
Jordan
```

## Cold Outreach to Hiring Manager

**Subject**: [Specific observation about their product or company]

Good subject line examples:
- `Your approach to [feature] is smart -- curious about what's next`
- `Notion API -> [their product category]: exploring PM roles`
- `[Recent launch/news] caught my attention`

**Body**:
```
Hi {{first_name}},

I've been following {{company_name}}'s work on [specific product/initiative].
[One specific observation about what they're doing well or an interesting
challenge they're tackling].

I led the API platform at Notion from private beta to 50K+ active
integrations, and before that redesigned onboarding at Figma (28% to 43%
activation). I'm exploring product leadership roles in [their space] and
think there's a strong fit.

Would you have 15 minutes next week?

Best,
Jordan
```

[recent news: launched a feature, raised funding, announced something].

your schedule.

Jordan

## Post-Offer Follow-Up (Requesting More Time)

**Subject**: Re: Offer -- next steps

**Body**:
```
Hi {{first_name}},

Thank you for the offer. I'm genuinely excited about {{company_name}} and
this role. I want to give it the thoughtful consideration it deserves.

Would it be possible to have until [date, typically 5-7 business days]
to make my decision? I want to make sure I'm fully committed when I say yes.

Happy to discuss any remaining questions in the meantime.

Jordan
```

## Key Tips for All Templates

1. **Subject line**: Be specific and genuine. Avoid spam-like phrasing.
2. **Length**: 3-4 short paragraphs, under 100 words for cold outreach.
3. **Personalization**: Every email must reference something unique to that person or company.
4. **CTA**: End with a specific, low-friction ask ("15 minutes next week").
5. **Sign-off**: Keep it simple -- "Best," "Thanks," or just your name.
6. **Timing**: Send during business hours in their timezone. Tuesday-Thursday gets best open rates.
7. **Don't attach resume**: Offer to share it if they express interest. An unsolicited attachment feels transactional.

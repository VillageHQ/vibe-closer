# Lead Discovery & Opportunity Sourcing

This document outlines where to find job opportunities that match Jordan's target company profiles and goals.

---

## Primary Sources

### 1. Your Network (Highest Priority)
- Use {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} to find connections at target companies
- Ask your network for warm intros to companies and roles you're targeting
- Warm intros have the highest conversion rate -- prioritize these above all other channels
- Check second-degree connections: who do your former Notion and Figma colleagues know?
- Tap investor and advisor networks from previous companies

### 2. Meeting Notes & Conversations
- Review conversations from the last 2 weeks ({{NOTETAKER}})
- Look for: company mentions, referral opportunities, warm intros offered
- Note colleagues who've moved to target companies (especially dev tools or productivity SaaS)
- Extract names and companies for follow-up research
- Pay attention to offhand mentions: "You should talk to..." is often the best lead

### 3. Email Inbox
- Scan inbound emails from the last 2 weeks ({{EMAIL_INBOX}})
- Look for: recruiter outreach, intro emails, referral messages, networking invitations
- Don't ignore recruiter emails even if the role isn't perfect -- they often have unlisted roles
- Check for connections sharing job opportunities or company news

### 4. Company Career Pages
Check career pages for companies matching your TCPs:
- Filter by "Product" or "Product Management" roles
- Look for Head of Product, VP of Product, or Senior PM titles
- Note posting date and seniority level
- Read job descriptions carefully for team and company insights
- Check "About Us" and "Culture" pages for fit signals

### 5. Job Boards & Aggregators
- **LinkedIn Jobs**: Set up saved searches for "Head of Product" + "developer tools" and "Senior PM" + "Series B-D"
- **Lenny's Newsletter Job Board**: High-quality product roles, curated
- **Y Combinator Work at a Startup**: Filter by product roles at Series B+ companies
- **Wellfound (AngelList)**: Filter by stage, sector, and role
- **Otta**: Curated tech roles with salary transparency
- **Key Values**: Filter by company culture attributes that matter to you
- **ProductHunt Jobs**: Often has roles at early-stage product companies

### 6. Company News & Funding Signals
- **Crunchbase**: Track funding rounds for companies in your TCP categories. Series B-D raises often trigger Head of Product hires.
- **TechCrunch / The Information**: Funding announcements, especially in dev tools and productivity
- **Company blogs**: Product announcements and "We're hiring" posts
- **LinkedIn company pages**: Follow target companies and enable notifications for job posts
- **HackerNews "Who is hiring?" threads**: Monthly threads with high-quality startup roles

### 7. Community & Networking
- **Lenny's Community**: Active product management community with job sharing
- **Product-Led Growth (PLG) Slack**: Community of PLG practitioners
- **DevTools FM**: Podcast community focused on developer tools
- **Local tech meetups**: SF and NYC product management and developer tools events
- **Industry conferences**: ProductCon, Mind the Product, developer tools events
- **Twitter/X**: Follow product leaders at target companies; engage with their content

### 8. Competitor & Adjacent Company Research
- If you admire a company, research their competitors -- they're often hiring too
- Check who's building in adjacent spaces (e.g., if targeting Notion, look at Coda, Slite, Almanac)
- Look at portfolio companies of VCs who invest in dev tools (Heavybit, Boldstart, Redpoint)

## Lead Qualification Criteria

An opportunity qualifies for your pipeline if:

**Role Fit**
- Matches one of your target roles (Head of Product, VP Product, Senior PM)
- Seniority level aligns with your 8 years of experience
- Product ownership scope is meaningful (not just feature PM)

**Company Fit**
- Matches at least one TCP (dev tools, productivity SaaS, or AI-native)
- Series B-D (or equivalent maturity for bootstrapped companies)
- 50-500 employees
- Product-led growth motion or strong product culture

**Contact Method**
- Identifiable hiring manager, recruiter, or internal connection
- Valid email or LinkedIn profile available
- Not already in your {{CRM_TRACKER}}

**Opportunity Signal**
- Role posted within last 2 weeks (or is an unlisted/new role)
- Active hiring indicated (not a "future pipeline" listing)
- From a trusted source (not spray-and-pray recruiter spam)

---

## Qualification Process

For each discovered opportunity:

1. **Verify Role & Company**: Read job description thoroughly, research the company
2. **Check TCP Fit**: Does it match your target company profiles?
3. **Find Contact**: Identify hiring manager, recruiter, or internal connection
4. **Look for Warm Path**: Do you have any connections? Use {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}}
5. **Assess Priority**: High (warm path + strong fit), Medium (cold but strong fit), Low (stretch or cold)
6. **Add to Pipeline**: Add to {{CRM_TRACKER}} with discovery source and priority

## Sourcing Tips

- **Set weekly sourcing time**: 3-4 hours per week dedicated to finding new opportunities
- **Track source quality**: Which sources yield the best reply rates? Double down on those.
- **Engage before outreach**: If you found someone through their content, engage with it first
- **Diversify sources**: Don't rely on job boards alone -- warm intros have the best conversion
- **Act quickly**: The best roles fill fast. Reach out within 2-3 days of discovering a strong fit.
- **Quality over quantity**: 5 well-researched, personalized outreaches beat 20 applications through a portal

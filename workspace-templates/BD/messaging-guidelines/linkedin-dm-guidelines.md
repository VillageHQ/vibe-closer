# LinkedIn for BD & Partnerships

## When to Use LinkedIn

- As a secondary channel after email (if no response in 5+ business days)
- When you have no email address but a clear LinkedIn profile for the partnerships contact
- When the recipient is active on LinkedIn (recent posts, comments, or shares)
- To warm up a cold contact by engaging with their content before emailing
- To reference a recent post or announcement as a timely conversation starter
- After a warm intro has been made, to connect and follow up

## Structure

- **Max 100 words** -- LinkedIn messages should be shorter and more conversational than email
- **Conversational tone** -- This is a chat, not a formal email; skip "Dear" and lengthy intros
- **Max 2-3 sentences** for first message -- brevity increases response rate
- **One clear ask** -- Keep it simple: a call, an email exchange, or a question

## Do
- Reference a specific recent post, product announcement, or company milestone
- Show you understand their product and market position
- Keep it casual and brief -- LinkedIn rewards conversational messages
- Ask a genuine question or propose a specific next step
- Use partnership/collaboration language, not sales language
- Engage with their content (like, comment) before sending a DM

## Don't
- Copy-paste your cold email into LinkedIn -- adapt the message for the format
- Send a connection request AND a pitch in the same message (connect first, pitch later)
- Write long-form partnership proposals on LinkedIn -- save details for email or a call
- Be vague about what type of partnership you're exploring
- Overuse emojis or overly casual language -- stay professional
- Message multiple people at the same company simultaneously via LinkedIn

## Connection Request Note (if not connected)

```
Hi {{first_name}} -- I lead partnerships at DevStack (developer portal platform).
I think there's an interesting integration opportunity between our products.
Would love to connect.
```

## Initial DM: Integration Partnership

```
Hey {{first_name}}, saw your recent post about [specific topic].
We're building DevStack, a developer portal used by 1,200+ teams.
Our customers keep asking for [their product category] data inline.
Curious if there's a partnership fit -- open to a quick call?
```

## Initial DM: Co-Sell Partnership

```
Hi {{first_name}}, noticed {{company_name}} is going after platform engineering teams.
We're seeing a lot of overlap with DevStack buyers. Might be worth exploring a co-sell motion.
Would you be open to a 20-minute chat?
```

## Follow-Up DM (After Email No-Reply)

```
Hi {{first_name}} -- sent you an email last week about a potential integration
between DevStack and {{company_name}}. Quick check: did it land?
Would love to connect if there's interest.
```

## Reference Their Recent Activity

```
Saw {{company_name}} just announced [feature/partnership/milestone].
That's closely aligned with what we're building at DevStack.
Been thinking about how our products could work together. Worth a conversation?
```

## After a Warm Intro

```
Hi {{first_name}} -- {{mutual_connection}} connected us. Thanks for accepting.
I'd love to chat about how DevStack and {{company_name}} might work together
for platform engineering teams. Free for 30 min next week?
```

## If They Respond Positively

Move the conversation to email or schedule a call immediately. LinkedIn is for opening doors, not negotiating terms. Suggested response:

```
Great to hear. Let me send you a quick email with more context and some
available times. What's the best email to reach you at?
```

## If They Don't Respond

- Don't follow up on LinkedIn more than once
- Wait at least 10 business days before trying a different contact at the same company
- Consider whether a warm intro path exists that you haven't tried

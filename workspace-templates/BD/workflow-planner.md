# Partnership Workflow Planner

## Outreach Sequencing Rules

### Core Principles
1. **Lead with mutual value** -- Always articulate what's in it for them, not just for you
2. **Reference complementary capabilities** -- Show you've researched their product/service and can articulate the specific integration or collaboration opportunity
3. **Propose specific collaboration ideas** -- Don't ask vague "partnership" questions; name the partnership type and what the first milestone looks like
4. **Use warm intros when available** -- Fetch paths via {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} and mention mutual connections. Warm intros are essential for BD: they cut response time in half and dramatically increase the chance of an exploratory call
5. **Respect decision-making cycles** -- Partnerships take longer than sales; follow the timing rules below and never pressure for speed
6. **Do your homework before outreach** -- Read their integrations page, recent blog posts, and product announcements. Reference something specific.

### Sequencing Rules
1. Identify strategic fit (research company, validate against IPP criteria in `profile/ipp.md`)
2. Find the right BD/partnerships contact (use {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} for warm paths first, then LinkedIn)
3. If warm intro available: request the intro and wait for it before cold outreach
4. If no warm intro: send mutual value pitch via email (cold outreach)
5. After 5 business days with no reply, send a targeted follow-up with a new angle
6. After 2 more business days, send a LinkedIn message as a secondary channel
7. After exploratory call, always follow up within 24 hours with a written summary and proposed next steps
8. If you are the last person to message, never follow up more than once per channel (email/LinkedIn) before waiting 7 business days
9. Partnership cycles are long -- expect 4-8 weeks from first contact to signed agreement

### Warm Intro Best Practices
- Always check {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} before any cold outreach
- When requesting an intro, provide a 2-sentence blurb the connector can forward
- Thank the connector and keep them updated on outcomes
- If the warm intro doesn't respond within 5 days, follow up with the connector once before trying a direct approach

## Pipeline Stages

1. **Target Identified** -- Prospect company identified as potential partner; meets IPP criteria
2. **Researched** -- Company researched; product/service reviewed; fit validated; specific partnership idea documented
3. **Contact Found** -- BD/partnerships contact identified and contact method obtained; warm intro path checked
4. **Outreach Sent** -- Initial partnership outreach email or warm intro request sent
5. **Exploratory Call** -- Call scheduled or completed to explore partnership potential
6. **Proposal/Co-Pitch** -- Formal partnership proposal or co-pitch presentation prepared and sent
7. **Negotiation** -- Terms being discussed; contract in progress
8. **Partnership Live** -- Partnership agreement signed and go-live initiated
9. **Passed** -- Decision made not to pursue (record reason in notes)

## Stage Transition Rules

- **Target Identified -> Researched**: After company research completed, IPP validation confirmed, and specific partnership idea documented
- **Researched -> Contact Found**: After finding valid contact email/LinkedIn and checking for warm intro paths
- **Contact Found -> Outreach Sent**: After partnership outreach sent or warm intro requested
- **Outreach Sent -> Exploratory Call**: After recipient replies and call is scheduled
- **Exploratory Call -> Proposal/Co-Pitch**: After call happens and both parties confirm mutual interest and agree on partnership type
- **Proposal/Co-Pitch -> Negotiation**: After proposal is sent and recipient signals willingness to negotiate terms
- **Negotiation -> Partnership Live**: After contract signed and launch plan confirmed
- **Any Stage -> Passed**: If partner declines or we decide to deprioritize (record reason)

## Follow-up Timing
- First follow-up: 5 business days after outreach (partnerships move slower than sales; give them space)
- Second follow-up: 7 business days after first follow-up (different angle, new information, or reference a recent announcement)
- LinkedIn follow-up: 2 business days after second email if no response on email
- Exploratory call follow-up: Within 24 hours with written summary and next steps
- Proposal response wait: 7-10 business days before follow-up (partnership decisions involve multiple stakeholders)
- Post-negotiation check-in: Weekly during active negotiation; don't let momentum die

## Weekly Partnership Review
Every Monday, review:
- Which prospects need follow-up this week?
- Which exploratory calls are scheduled?
- Are any proposals waiting for response past the 10-day mark?
- Are there new warm intro paths available for stalled prospects?
- What new partner prospects should be added from recent conversations or market signals?

# Ideal Partner Profiles (IPPs)

## IPP 1: Monitoring & Observability Platforms

### Company Profile
- **Company Size**: 200-2,000 employees; $20M-$500M ARR; Series C to public
- **Product/Service**: Application performance monitoring, infrastructure observability, log management, and alerting platforms for engineering teams
- **Market Position**: Established players with strong developer adoption; competing in a crowded but growing market where differentiation comes through integrations and developer experience

### Why Partnership Makes Sense
DevStack is where developers go to understand their services. Observability data (error rates, latency, deployment health) shown directly in the service catalog makes both products stickier. Developers already ask us for monitoring context when browsing service docs. This is one of the top-requested integration categories from our customer base.

### Partnership Type(s)
- [x] Integration (embed observability data in DevStack service pages)
- [ ] Co-Sell
- [ ] Distribution
- [x] Co-Marketing (joint webinars on developer experience + observability)

### Key Contact Role(s)
- VP/Director of Partnerships & Integrations
- Head of Ecosystem / Platform
- Director of Product (Integrations)

### Overlap & Complementary Capabilities
- They provide runtime visibility (metrics, traces, logs); we provide design-time context (docs, APIs, ownership, runbooks)
- Combined: a developer can see who owns a service, read its docs, check its health, and find the on-call -- all in one place
- Their customers are our customers: platform engineering teams at mid-market and enterprise companies

### Target Outreach Message Angle
"Your monitoring data is most valuable when developers can see it in context. DevStack surfaces service ownership, docs, and APIs alongside your metrics -- making both products indispensable to platform teams. Our customers are already asking for this."

### Deal Structure (if revenue-based)
- No direct revenue share for integration partnerships
- Joint co-marketing investment: shared webinar costs, co-branded content
- Potential co-sell referral fee (20% recurring) if we formalize mutual customer introductions

## IPP 2: CI/CD Platforms

### Company Profile
- **Company Size**: 100-1,500 employees; $10M-$300M ARR; Series B to public
- **Product/Service**: Continuous integration and continuous deployment platforms that help engineering teams build, test, and ship software faster
- **Market Position**: Growing platforms competing on developer experience, speed, and reliability; increasingly focused on platform engineering and internal developer platform narratives

### Why Partnership Makes Sense
CI/CD is a natural complement to DevStack. Developers who discover services in our portal immediately want to know: what's the build status? When was this last deployed? Can I trigger a deploy? Surfacing CI/CD pipeline status and deployment history inside DevStack service pages creates a seamless inner-loop experience. Co-sell is also strong here: platform engineering teams buying DevStack are often evaluating or upgrading their CI/CD at the same time.

### Partnership Type(s)
- [x] Integration (surface build/deploy status in DevStack)
- [x] Co-Sell (joint pitches to platform engineering teams)
- [ ] Distribution
- [x] Co-Marketing (joint content on internal developer platforms)

### Key Contact Role(s)
- VP of Business Development / Partnerships
- Head of Integrations
- Director of Product (Ecosystem)
- VP of Sales (for co-sell alignment)

### Overlap & Complementary Capabilities
- They own the build-and-deploy pipeline; we own the discover-and-understand layer
- Together: a developer finds a service in DevStack, sees its docs, checks its last deployment, and triggers a new build -- without leaving the portal
- Shared buyer: VP/Director of Platform Engineering or Developer Experience

### Target Outreach Message Angle
"Platform engineering teams are buying DevStack and CI/CD together. We're seeing consistent demand for CI/CD context inside our portal. Let's explore a joint integration and co-sell motion -- your pipeline data in our portal, and mutual customer introductions."

### Deal Structure (if revenue-based)
- Co-sell: 20% recurring revenue share on mutual referrals (both directions)
- Minimum 3 joint customer introductions per quarter
- Integration partnership: no direct fee; mutual engineering investment
- Co-marketing: shared costs for webinars and conference presence

## IPP 3: Cloud Provider Marketplaces

### Company Profile
- **Company Size**: Large (10,000+ employees) -- these are the major cloud providers
- **Product/Service**: Cloud infrastructure, compute, storage, and managed services; each operates a marketplace for third-party developer tools
- **Market Position**: Market leaders (AWS, GCP, Azure) with massive distribution through their marketplaces and partner programs

### Why Partnership Makes Sense
Cloud marketplaces are the fastest-growing distribution channel for developer tools. Listing DevStack on AWS Marketplace, GCP Marketplace, and Azure Marketplace would unlock procurement-friendly purchasing (customers use committed cloud spend), reduce sales friction, and increase discoverability among enterprise buyers who default to marketplace procurement. This is a distribution play, not a product integration.

### Partnership Type(s)
- [ ] Integration
- [ ] Co-Sell
- [x] Distribution (marketplace listing and partner program enrollment)
- [x] Co-Marketing (featured in marketplace categories and partner spotlights)

### Key Contact Role(s)
- Partner Development Manager (specific to developer tools category)
- ISV Partner Manager
- Marketplace Operations contact
- Solutions Architect (for technical validation)

### Overlap & Complementary Capabilities
- They provide infrastructure and procurement channels; we provide a developer experience layer that runs on their cloud
- DevStack customers already run on these clouds -- marketplace listing removes procurement friction
- Cloud providers want to feature strong developer experience tools to differentiate their ecosystem

### Target Outreach Message Angle
"DevStack helps engineering teams get more value from their cloud investment by consolidating developer tooling into a single portal. We're already deployed on [cloud provider] by 500+ teams. A marketplace listing would simplify procurement for our mutual customers and help your developer tools category."

### Deal Structure (if revenue-based)
- Standard marketplace revenue share (typically 3-5% to cloud provider)
- Co-marketing: inclusion in partner directories, case studies, and marketplace featured listings
- Potential for cloud credits or co-sell incentives through partner programs (e.g., AWS ISV Accelerate, Google Cloud Partner Advantage)

# Tone Guidelines

Your voice with investors should be confident, founder-to-founder/investor-to-investor, and narrative-driven. You're not selling; you're inviting them into an opportunity.

## Voice
- **Confident, not arrogant**: You know what you're building and why it matters. Don't be humble about your vision or traction.
- **Founder-to-investor**: Speak peer-to-peer. You're solving a real problem; they have capital. It's a partnership opportunity.
- **Narrative-driven**: Connect the dots for them. Problem -> Solution -> Traction -> Vision. Make it coherent and compelling.
- **Authentic**: Use your real voice. Don't sound like a pitch deck. Let your passion come through.

## Do
- **Reference their portfolio**: "I loved your investment in [company] because [specific reason]" shows you did homework.
- **Mention specific thesis alignment**: "Your thesis on [industry trend] aligns perfectly with what we're building."
- **Lead with traction, not pitch**: "$180K ARR with zero churn and 3x MoM growth" is better than "We're disrupting customer support."
- **Keep warm intro emails short**: Let the mutual connection's credibility do the heavy lifting. You're just context.
- **Show conviction**: "We believe every support team will have an AI copilot within 3 years — we're building that future" comes across better than "Some people think AI might help support teams."
- **Use specific, not generic language**: "We reduce ticket resolution time by 60% for mid-market support teams" beats "We're an AI company improving customer service."
- **Be direct**: Ask for what you want. "Would you be open to a 20-minute call next week?" is clear and respectful.
- **Quantify everything**: "$180K ARR, 28 customers, 60% faster resolution" is more credible than "fast-growing with strong traction."

## Don't
- **Start with "I hope this email finds you well"**: Investors get hundreds of these. Jump straight to the interesting part.
- **Use hype words or buzzwords**: "Disruptive," "AI-powered," "next-generation" — show, don't tell.
- **Claim you're "the Uber of..."**: Own your space. Be specific about what you do and why it's needed.
- **Make the email about yourself**: It's about why they should care. Lead with the opportunity, not your background.
- **Over-explain in the first touch**: Your goal is a meeting, not a closed deal. Give them enough to be intrigued.
- **Use multiple exclamation marks**: One per email maximum. Sound calm and confident.
- **Copy-paste templates**: Every investor outreach should feel personalized to that investor's thesis and portfolio.
- **Lead with ask**: Don't open with "Would you like to invest?" Open with why you're reaching out.
- **Mention competitors by name in outreach**: Focus on your own story. Competitors come up in meetings, not emails.

## Structure of a Good Outreach Email

1. **Context** (1 sentence): Why you're reaching out to THIS investor specifically
2. **Hook** (1-2 sentences): What you're building and why it matters
3. **Proof** (1 sentence): Traction or validation that it's working
4. **Ask** (1 sentence): Clear call-to-action (usually: meeting)

**Total**: 4-5 sentences, under 100 words.

## Examples of Good Tone

### Example 1: Warm Intro Follow-Up (Worked)

**Setup**: Warm intro from a portfolio founder at a seed-stage AI fund. Partner responded within 2 hours.

**Email**:
> Hi Sarah — thanks for connecting with me through Alex. We're building Lumina AI, an AI copilot that sits inside Zendesk/Intercom and helps support agents resolve tickets 60% faster.
>
> We're at $180K ARR with 28 customers and zero churn — the product is genuinely sticky because it learns from each agent's corrections and gets better over time.
>
> Given your investment in [similar portfolio company], I think you'd find our approach to the support AI space interesting. Would you be open to a 20-minute call this week?

**Result**: Call booked same day, led to partner meeting.

### Example 2: Cold Email That Converted

**Setup**: No warm path. Investor had written a blog post about AI in enterprise workflows.

**Email**:
> Hi David — your recent post on "why vertical AI wins" resonated with me. We're proving that thesis in customer support.
>
> Lumina AI helps support teams resolve tickets 60% faster. We're at $180K ARR, growing 3x MoM, with zero churn across 28 customers.
>
> I built the ML ticket routing system at Zendesk that handles 500M+ tickets/year — now we're building the AI layer that should've existed on top of it.
>
> Would love to share more. Open to a call next week?

**Result**: Replied in 24 hours, booked meeting.

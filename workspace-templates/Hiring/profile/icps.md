# Ideal Candidate Profiles

## ICP 1: "Senior Backend Engineer — Infra/Platform"

### Target
- **Seniority**: Senior / Staff (5-10 years experience)
- **Background**: Distributed systems, infrastructure, or platform engineering
- **Current employers**: Companies like Stripe, Datadog, Vercel, Cloudflare, HashiCorp, or similar infra companies
- **Tech stack**: Go or Rust preferred, strong with Kubernetes, familiar with CI/CD at scale
- **Geography**: Remote (US/Canada/Europe timezones)

### Why They'd Join
They're at a big company where their impact is diluted. They want to own a critical system end-to-end at a company where infra IS the product, not a cost center. The 3x growth means they'd be building for scale, not maintaining legacy systems.

### Pain Points at Current Role
1. Slow decision-making — RFCs take months, not days
2. Limited ownership — they maintain a slice of a larger system, don't own the full stack
3. Impact feels invisible — their work ships to internal teams, not directly to customers

### Our Pitch to This ICP
"You've built infra at [company] that serves millions. At Buildkraft, you'd own the core pipeline engine that processes 2M+ deployments daily for 340+ engineering teams. We're 14 engineers, Series A, growing 3x — your work would be the product, not overhead."

### Where to Find Them
- LinkedIn: "Senior Backend Engineer" OR "Staff Engineer" at Stripe, Datadog, Vercel, Cloudflare
- GitHub: Contributors to open-source CI/CD tools (Dagger, Earthly, BuildKit)
- Communities: CNCF Slack, KubeCon attendees, r/devops, Hacker News "Who wants to be hired" threads

### Signals They're Ready to Move
- Updated LinkedIn in last 30 days
- Started posting about side projects or "open to opportunities"
- Company just did layoffs or reorg
- Been in current role 2-3+ years (plateau zone)

## ICP 2: "Senior Frontend Engineer — Data Visualization"

### Target
- **Seniority**: Senior (4-8 years experience)
- **Background**: Complex data-heavy UIs, dashboards, or developer tools
- **Current employers**: Grafana, Datadog, Vercel, Linear, or similar dev tools companies
- **Tech stack**: React/TypeScript, D3 or similar viz libraries, real-time data experience
- **Geography**: Remote (US/Canada/Europe timezones)

### Why They'd Join
They're building dashboards at a company where frontend is secondary to the backend. They want to be at a company where the dashboard IS the product experience — where frontend excellence directly drives revenue.

### Pain Points at Current Role
1. Frontend is treated as "skinning the API" — no real product influence
2. Design system is locked down, limited creative/technical challenge
3. Performance work is reactive (fix jank) not proactive (build for scale)

### Our Pitch to This ICP
"We're building a real-time deployment analytics dashboard from scratch — think Datadog-quality visualization for CI/CD pipelines. You'd own the entire frontend for a product used by 340+ engineering teams. React, TypeScript, real-time WebSocket data, and actual complex viz challenges."

### Where to Find Them
- LinkedIn: "Senior Frontend Engineer" at Grafana, Datadog, Linear, Vercel
- GitHub: Contributors to D3, Recharts, or developer tool UIs
- Communities: React conf attendees, Frontend Masters instructors, Twitter/X frontend community

## ICP 3: "ML Engineer — Infrastructure Optimization"

### Target
- **Seniority**: Mid-Senior (3-7 years experience)
- **Background**: Applied ML for systems/infrastructure, not pure research
- **Current employers**: Cloud providers (AWS, GCP), infra companies, or ML platform companies
- **Tech stack**: Python, PyTorch/TensorFlow, experience with production ML systems
- **Geography**: Remote (US/Canada/Europe timezones)

### Why They'd Join
They want to apply ML to real infrastructure problems at scale, not build yet another recommendation engine. Our multi-cloud orchestration challenge is genuinely novel — optimizing deployment routing across providers based on cost, latency, and reliability.

### Our Pitch to This ICP
"We're building an ML-powered multi-cloud orchestration layer — think 'smart routing for deployments' across AWS, GCP, and Azure. Real optimization problem with 2M+ daily deployments as training data. Greenfield project, you'd be the founding ML engineer."

### Where to Find Them
- LinkedIn: "ML Engineer" OR "Applied Scientist" at AWS, GCP, Databricks, Anyscale
- Papers: Authors of papers on resource optimization, scheduling, or systems ML
- Communities: MLOps Community Slack, NeurIPS Systems for ML workshop attendees

# Tone Guidelines

## Voice
- **Engineer-to-engineer, not recruiter-to-candidate** — Write like a technical peer who happens to be hiring, not a recruiter with a quota
- **Specific and honest** — Lead with the actual technical challenge, not vague "exciting opportunity" language
- **Respectful of their time** — Short, direct, no fluff. They get 10 recruiter emails a week; yours should feel different
- **Confident but not desperate** — We have a great company; we're looking for the right person, not begging anyone

## Do
- Lead with the specific technical problem they'd work on ("We're building a deployment router that handles 2M+ deploys/day")
- Mention something specific about THEIR background ("Saw your work on BuildKit...")
- Keep cold outreach under 100 words
- Use their first name
- Be transparent about comp range and stage
- If you have a mutual connection, mention them in the first sentence

## Don't
- Never open with "I came across your profile" — everyone says that
- Never say "exciting opportunity" or "fast-paced environment"
- Never list every tech in your stack like a job posting
- Don't use exclamation marks in cold outreach
- Don't ask "Are you open to new opportunities?" — it's implied
- Don't send the same message to backend and frontend candidates

## Tone Examples

### Good
> "Hi Alex — your BuildKit contributions caught my eye. We're building the deployment layer for 340+ engineering teams at Buildkraft (Series A, $4.2M ARR). The core challenge right now: scaling our pipeline engine from 2M to 10M daily deployments. Would love to share more if you're curious."

### Bad
> "Hi Alex! I'm a recruiter at Buildkraft, an exciting startup that's transforming the CI/CD space. We're looking for talented backend engineers to join our amazing team. With our cutting-edge technology and fast-paced environment, this is a unique opportunity to make an impact. Would you be open to a quick chat?"

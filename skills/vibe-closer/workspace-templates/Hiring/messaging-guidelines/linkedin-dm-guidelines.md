# LinkedIn DM Guidelines — Hiring

## When to Use
- When you have no email for the candidate
- When candidate is active on LinkedIn (posting, commenting recently)
- As a follow-up channel after email gets no reply (Day 7)
- When their LinkedIn shows "Open to Work" or recent activity suggesting a move

## Structure
- Max 60 words for connection request note
- Max 100 words for first DM after connecting
- Lead with something specific about them (a post, a project, their background)
- One clear next step — "worth a quick call?" not a full pitch

## Connection Request Note
"Hi {{first_name}} — saw your {{specific_work — e.g., 'BuildKit contributions' or 'talk at KubeCon on scheduling'}}. Building the deployment infra layer at Buildkraft and think you'd find the technical challenges interesting. Would love to connect."

## First DM (After Connected)
"Thanks for connecting, {{first_name}}. Quick context — we're {{one-liner about role challenge, e.g., 'scaling our pipeline engine from 2M to 10M daily deploys'}} and your background in {{their_expertise}} is exactly what we need. Worth a 20-min call to share more?"

## Engaging Before Outreach
- If the candidate posts technical content, leave a thoughtful comment (not "Great post!")
- If they share an article, add a genuine insight
- Do this 1-2 days before sending a connection request — it makes the request feel warmer

## Don't
- Don't send InMail — connection request + DM converts better
- Don't copy-paste the email into LinkedIn (different channel, different tone)
- Don't pitch comp or benefits in the DM — save for the call
- Don't send messages longer than 3 sentences
- Don't connect with multiple people at the same company simultaneously (looks spammy)

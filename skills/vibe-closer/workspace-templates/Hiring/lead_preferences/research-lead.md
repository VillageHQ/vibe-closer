# Lead Research Strategy

How to research a candidate before outreach. Follow these steps in order, using available MCPs.

## Research Steps

1. **Profile enrichment** via `{{PROFILE_ENRICHMENT}}`:
   - Full LinkedIn profile: current role, tenure, career trajectory
   - Technical skills, certifications, and domain expertise
   - Recent activity — posts, articles, or engagement that reveals interests
   - "Open to Work" signals or recent profile updates

2. **Technical footprint** via `{{WEBSITE_CRAWLING}}`:
   - GitHub profile: contributions, projects, languages, activity level
   - Personal blog or portfolio site
   - Conference talks, published papers, or podcast appearances
   - Open-source contributions to tools relevant to your stack

3. **ICP matching** — cross-reference findings with `profile/icps.md`:
   - Which candidate profile does this person match?
   - What aspects of your role/company would appeal to them most?
   - What might their concerns or objections be?

4. **Warm path check** via `{{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}}`:
   - Mutual connections who could vouch or make introductions
   - Former colleagues at your company or in your network
   - Shared communities, meetups, or alumni networks

## Output

Summarize research as:
- **Key findings**: 3-5 bullet points about the candidate's background and fit
- **Personalization hooks**: specific details to reference in outreach (e.g., "your talk on distributed systems at GopherCon," "your contributions to the Kubernetes operator SDK")
- **Role fit**: which aspects of the role align with their experience and interests
- **Warm paths**: mutual connections or shared context
- **Recommended approach**: which template to use, what angle to lead with

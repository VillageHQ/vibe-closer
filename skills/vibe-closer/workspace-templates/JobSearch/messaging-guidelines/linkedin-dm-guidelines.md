# LinkedIn DM Guidelines

LinkedIn is a **secondary channel** in your job search workflow. Use it strategically to amplify your outreach, build relationships, and follow up when email doesn't land.

---

## When to Use LinkedIn DM

**Do use LinkedIn when:**
- You have no email address for the hiring manager or recruiter
- They're very active on LinkedIn (recent posts, comments, or shares)
- You're following up on an email with no response (3-5 days later)
- You want to build rapport before asking for an intro (engage with their content first)
- The company culture is social-media native (startup, tech)
- You want to connect with employees for networking before applying

**Don't use LinkedIn when:**
- You have a valid email address and haven't tried email first
- The company is traditional or formal
- You haven't done any research on the company or person
- You're planning to send the same message to multiple people at the same company

---

## Message Structure

- **Length**: Max 100 words (shorter than email -- this is a chat)
- **Tone**: Conversational and professional, not corporate or stiff
- **No subject line** -- it's a direct message
- **No formal sign-off** -- just your name or no signature

---

## Connection Request Note (if not connected)

```
Hi {{first_name}} -- I've been following {{company_name}}'s work in
[specific area]. Exploring product leadership roles in dev tools and
would love to connect.
```

**After they accept**, follow up with a DM:

```
Thanks for connecting! I'm exploring Head of Product roles in [space]
and I'm genuinely impressed by {{company_name}}'s approach to [specific
product feature]. Would you be open to a quick coffee chat?
```

## Engaging Before Asking

**Best practice**: Build familiarity before sending a cold DM.

1. Like and comment meaningfully on their last 2-3 posts (not just "Great post!")
2. Send a connection request with a brief, non-pitchy note
3. Wait for them to accept
4. **Then** send a DM with a real ask

This increases reply rates significantly. People are more likely to respond to someone they recognize.

---

## If They Respond Positively

Move the conversation to email or schedule a call. LinkedIn is for opening doors, not having extended conversations.

```
That's great to hear. Would you prefer I send more context via email
or should we jump on a quick call? Happy either way.
```

## If They Don't Reply

- Don't follow up on LinkedIn more than once
- Wait at least 7 days before trying a different person at the same company
- Move on and revisit later if new signals emerge (new role posted, funding round, etc.)

## If They Reply with a Question

- Answer directly in 1-2 sentences
- End with your ask: "Would be great to chat more -- you open to a quick call?"

## LinkedIn Profile Optimization for Job Search

While DMing, make sure your profile supports your outreach:

- **Headline**: Include your target role and domain -- "Product Leader | Dev Tools & PLG | Ex-Notion, Figma"
- **About Section**: Your candidate brief summary (concise, outcome-focused)
- **Featured Section**: Link to a portfolio, writing samples, or a recent project
- **Recent Activity**: Engage with content in your target space -- shows your thinking and interests
- **Open to Work**: Enable this so recruiters can find you (choose visible to recruiters only if preferred)

# Workflow Planner

## Outreach Sequencing Rules

### Core Principles
1. **Network before applying** -- A warm intro or internal referral is 10x more effective than a cold application. Always check for connections before submitting a resume through a portal.
2. **Research First**: Before reaching out, understand the company's product, recent launches, challenges, and strategy. Read their blog, try their product, follow their leaders on social.
3. **Personalize Always**: Reference specific company initiatives, products, or news relevant to the role. Generic outreach gets ignored.
4. **Warm Intro Priority**: Fetch paths using {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} -- warm introductions are the PRIMARY channel for job search. A referral from a mutual connection carries more weight than any cold email.
5. **Cold Outreach Strategy** (when no warm path exists):
   - Lead with what you know about the company, not what you need
   - Connect your experience directly to their stated challenges or open roles
   - Keep it concise (under 100 words)
6. **Follow-Up Cadence**: If no reply after 3-5 business days, send one follow-up with a new angle
   - If still no reply after 5 more days, try LinkedIn as a secondary channel
   - Max 2 follow-ups per contact before moving on
7. **Always Next-Step**: After any conversation, confirm the next step and timing in writing
8. **Interview Prep**: Once scheduled, research the interviewer's background, prepare relevant examples, and practice articulating your experience

### Warm Intro Best Practices
- Always check {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} before any direct outreach to a company
- When requesting an intro, make it easy for the connector: provide a 2-sentence blurb they can forward
- Thank the connector regardless of outcome
- Don't cold-email someone at a company where you've already requested a warm intro (it undercuts the connector)
- Follow up with the connector if the intro doesn't get a response within 5 days

### Networking Before Applying
- For every target company, first ask: "Do I know anyone there, or anyone who knows someone there?"
- Networking conversations are not asks for a job -- they're conversations about the company, the market, and the role
- The goal of a networking call is to learn, build rapport, and potentially get referred -- not to pitch yourself
- After a good networking conversation, the other person should want to refer you without being asked

## Pipeline Stages

1. **Target Company** -- Company identified as fit based on TCP criteria, added to tracker
2. **Researched** -- Company research complete (product, culture, recent news, open roles, team structure)
3. **Contact Found** -- Hiring manager, recruiter, or internal connection identified with valid email/LinkedIn
4. **Outreach Sent** -- Initial message sent (warm intro requested, cold email, or application submitted)
5. **Replied** -- Recipient responded positively or asked clarifying questions
6. **Phone Screen** -- Scheduled and/or completed initial phone conversation
7. **Interview** -- Formal interview process in progress (one or more rounds)
8. **Offer** -- Offer extended
9. **Accepted** -- Offer accepted, position secured
10. **Declined** -- Position declined or not advanced (record reason)

## Stage Transition Rules

- **Target Company -> Researched**: When company research is complete and at least one relevant open role confirmed
- **Researched -> Contact Found**: When you have a verified hiring manager, recruiter, or warm contact with a way to reach them
- **Contact Found -> Outreach Sent**: When initial message is sent or warm intro is requested
- **Outreach Sent -> Replied**: When you receive any response (positive, clarifying, or scheduling)
- **Replied -> Phone Screen**: When a conversation or call is scheduled
- **Phone Screen -> Interview**: When moved to formal interview process (received interview loop details)
- **Interview -> Offer**: When offer is extended (verbal or written)
- **Offer -> Accepted/Declined**: When final decision is made

## Follow-up Timing
- After cold email: 3-5 business days for first follow-up
- After first follow-up: 5 business days, then try LinkedIn
- After warm intro request: 3 business days to check in with connector
- After phone screen: Same day or next morning, send thank-you with specific reference to conversation
- After interview round: Within 24 hours, send thoughtful thank-you to each interviewer
- After final round: Ask about timeline for decision; follow up once if past their stated date

## Rules & Best Practices

- **Personalization is Non-Negotiable**: Every outreach must reference something specific about the company or role
- **Track Everything**: Log all interactions, next steps, and follow-up dates in {{CRM_TRACKER}}
- **Respect Timeline**: Don't contact same person more than once per channel per week
- **Quality Over Quantity**: 5 well-researched outreaches beat 20 generic applications
- **Build Relationships**: Focus on building genuine connections, not just landing interviews
- **Document Lessons**: After phone screens or interviews, note key insights, questions asked, and what went well or didn't
- **Batch Similar Work**: Do all research in one block, all outreach in another. Context-switching kills quality.

## Interview Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Research interviewer(s) on LinkedIn -- their background, what they've built, what they care about
- [ ] Review company's product thoroughly -- sign up, use it, form genuine opinions
- [ ] Read recent blog posts, press releases, and leadership interviews
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 specific examples using STAR method tailored to this role
- [ ] Have 3-5 thoughtful questions ready that show you've done homework (not things easily found on the website)
- [ ] Confirm timing, format (phone/video), and participant names/roles
- [ ] Test technology if virtual (camera, mic, internet)
- [ ] Plan travel/timing if in-person
- [ ] Prepare a brief product critique or strategic observation about their product (useful for product leadership roles)
- [ ] Review your candidate brief to ensure consistency in how you present your story

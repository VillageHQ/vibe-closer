# Sequence Flow

> Messaging tone, email rules, and LinkedIn rules → `messaging-guidelines/`
> Pipeline stages and transition rules → `pipeline-config.md` → `## Pipeline Stages`

## Execution Sequence

For each lead, follow these steps:

1. Fetch warm paths via {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} — always prioritize warm intros and networking before cold outreach
2. Send initial outreach via the 1st path — warm intro or referral first; cold outreach only when no warm path exists
3. If no reply after 5 days, send follow-up (in recipient's timezone)
4. If no reply after 5 more days, try LinkedIn as a secondary channel
5. Always draft a reply and follow-up on the last message
6. If the last message is from me, never follow-up more than once per channel
7. Max 2 follow-ups per contact before moving on
8. If the lead replies and is not interested, mark as Declined in {{CRM_TRACKER}}
9. If no reply after completing all paths, set follow-up date to 6 months from now
10. If still no reply after the 6-month follow-up, set follow-up date to 1 year from now

## Frequency Rules

- Max 1 contact per channel per week
- Post-phone screen: same-day or next-morning thank-you
- Post-interview round: within 24 hours, thank-you to each interviewer
- After final round: ask about timeline for decision; follow up once if past their stated date

## Key Principles

- **Network before applying** — warm intro/referral is 10x more effective than a cold application.
- **Don't cold-email where you've requested a warm intro** — it undercuts the connector.
- **Networking conversations are about learning, not pitching** — after a good call, the other person should want to refer you without being asked.

## Interview Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Research interviewer(s) on LinkedIn — background, what they've built, what they care about
- [ ] Review company's product — sign up, use it, form genuine opinions
- [ ] Read recent blog posts, press releases, and leadership interviews
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 STAR examples tailored to this role
- [ ] Have 3-5 thoughtful questions that show homework (not things on the website)
- [ ] Confirm timing, format, and participant names/roles
- [ ] Test technology if virtual (camera, mic, internet)
- [ ] Review your candidate brief for consistency in how you present your story

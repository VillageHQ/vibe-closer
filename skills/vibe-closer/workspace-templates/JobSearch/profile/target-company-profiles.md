# Target Company Profiles

## TCP 1: Growth-Stage Developer Tools

### Company Type
Series B-D startups building tools for software engineers, platform teams, or DevOps practitioners.

### Industry/Sector
Developer tools, developer infrastructure, platform engineering, DevOps, and internal tooling.

### Size
100-500 employees. $10M-$80M ARR. Post-PMF but still in the rapid scaling phase where product decisions have outsized impact.

### Culture & Values
Engineering-driven culture with high technical bar. Values craft, user empathy, and shipping speed. Writing culture (RFCs, design docs, memos over meetings). Low ego, high accountability. Often founder-led with technical co-founders.

### Target Role(s)
- Head of Product
- VP of Product
- Senior PM / Lead PM (if it's the most senior product role)

### Why This Attracts You
Developer tools are where I've built my career and where I have the deepest conviction. I understand the buyer (developer), the adoption motion (bottom-up, self-serve), and the GTM dynamics (community, docs, and trust matter more than sales decks). At this stage, I can shape the product vision and build the product team while the company scales from $10M to $50M+ ARR.

### How Your Skills Map
- 8 years building products for developers (API platforms, SDKs, collaboration tools)
- Deep experience with PLG motions that drive bottom-up adoption and expansion
- Track record of building PM teams from scratch (1 to 5 PMs at Notion)
- Understand platform thinking: APIs, extensibility, ecosystem dynamics

### Typical Pain Points
- Transitioning from founder-led product to a structured product org
- Balancing developer experience with enterprise requirements (SSO, compliance, admin controls)
- Scaling self-serve while building an enterprise sales motion
- Prioritizing across platform, core product, and growth initiatives

### Where to Find Them
- Y Combinator company directory (filter by developer tools, Series B+)
- Crunchbase funding alerts for developer tools companies
- Heavybit portfolio and community
- DevTools FM podcast guest companies
- GitHub trending and ProductHunt developer tools launches
- KubeCon, DevOpsDays, and PlatformCon sponsor lists

## TCP 2: Productivity & Collaboration SaaS

### Company Type
Series C+ companies building productivity, collaboration, or knowledge management tools with strong product-led growth motions.

### Industry/Sector
Productivity software, team collaboration, knowledge management, project management, and workflow automation.

### Size
200-800 employees. $30M-$150M ARR. Established product-market fit with a growing enterprise motion layered on top of self-serve.

### Culture & Values
Product-obsessed, design-forward, and user-centric. Values simplicity and craft in product experience. Often has a strong content/community presence. Collaborative but opinionated -- the product team has a real voice in company strategy.

### Target Role(s)
- Head of Product (specific product area: collaboration, growth, platform)
- Senior PM / Staff PM on a high-leverage product area

### Why This Attracts You
I spent formative years at Notion and Figma -- both defining examples of this category. I deeply understand what makes productivity tools succeed: elegant defaults, progressive disclosure, viral team adoption, and expansion from individual to team to enterprise. This is a space where product craft directly drives business outcomes, and where my PLG expertise has the most leverage.

### How Your Skills Map
- Direct experience at two category-defining productivity tools (Notion, Figma)
- Led activation and onboarding optimization that drove 15-point improvement in 7-day activation
- Built expansion revenue features that moved NRR from 115% to 138%
- Understand the individual-to-team-to-enterprise adoption curve firsthand

### Typical Pain Points
- Maintaining product simplicity while adding enterprise features
- Balancing organic/PLG growth with outbound sales pressure
- Building AI features that feel native, not bolted on
- Managing the tension between horizontal platform and vertical use cases
- Retaining product culture while scaling the organization

### Where to Find Them
- SaaStr portfolio and event sponsors
- Product-Led Growth community (productled.org)
- Bessemer Cloud Index (filter by collaboration/productivity)
- LinkedIn Jobs filtered by "productivity" + "Head of Product"
- Lenny's Newsletter job board
- First Round Capital portfolio

## TCP 3: AI-Native Developer Infrastructure (Stretch)

### Company Type
Series A-C companies building AI-powered tools for developers -- code generation, testing, deployment, or developer workflow automation.

### Industry/Sector
AI/ML infrastructure, AI-powered developer tools, code intelligence, automated testing, and AI-assisted workflows.

### Size
30-200 employees. $5M-$30M ARR. Earlier stage, higher risk, but potentially category-defining.

### Culture & Values
Technical, fast-moving, and experimental. Comfortable with ambiguity. Often founded by ex-FAANG or ex-research engineers. Values technical depth and first-principles thinking. May be less polished on process but strong on intellectual rigor.

### Target Role(s)
- Head of Product (first product hire or first senior product hire)
- VP of Product

### Why This Attracts You
This is the stretch category. AI is reshaping developer tools in real time, and I want exposure to that wave. My platform and developer tools background translates directly -- the core challenge is still "make developers more productive" -- but the product paradigm is shifting. This is where the next Notion or Figma-scale opportunity might come from, and I'd rather be early than late.

### How Your Skills Map
- Deep developer tools domain expertise transfers directly
- Experience building platform products with API/extensibility layers
- Strong at zero-to-one product development (launched Notion's API from scratch)
- Comfortable with ambiguity and rapid iteration

### Typical Pain Points
- Defining product direction in a fast-evolving AI landscape
- Building user trust in AI-generated outputs (accuracy, reliability)
- Pricing and packaging for AI-powered tools (usage-based vs. seat-based)
- Transitioning from technical demo to productized, scalable offering
- Hiring product people who understand both AI and developer audiences

### Where to Find Them
- AI Grant portfolio companies
- Sequoia Arc and AI-focused VC portfolios
- HackerNews "Show HN" posts for AI developer tools
- AI Engineer conference sponsors and speakers
- Twitter/X AI developer tools community
- Crunchbase funding alerts filtered for AI + developer tools

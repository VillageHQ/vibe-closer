# Ideal Startup Profiles

## ICP 1: AI-Native B2B SaaS — Early Revenue

- **Stage**: Seed ($500K-$2M round)
- **Traction**: $100K-$500K ARR, 10-30 customers, growing 15%+ MoM
- **Team**: Technical founder(s) with domain expertise, 3-10 person team
- **Market**: Enterprise workflow where AI replaces manual processes (compliance, data ops, security, DevOps, legal)
- **Product**: AI is the core architecture, not a feature. Clear technical moat (proprietary data, fine-tuned models, novel inference approach)
- **Why we invest**: Product-market fit signal from early customers, technical differentiation, large enterprise TAM

## ICP 2: Technical Infrastructure — Pre-Revenue

- **Stage**: Pre-Seed ($500K-$1M round)
- **Traction**: Design partners, LOIs, or waitlist. May have pilot revenue <$50K
- **Team**: Exceptional technical founders — ex-FAANG, open-source maintainers, PhD researchers with shipping experience
- **Market**: Developer tools, data infrastructure, ML/AI infrastructure, security tooling
- **Product**: Solves a pain point the founder experienced firsthand. Clear path to enterprise adoption
- **Why we invest**: Founder-market fit is exceptional, market timing is right, willing to bet on the team pre-revenue

## ICP 3: Vertical SaaS — AI-Powered

- **Stage**: Seed ($1M-$2M round)
- **Traction**: $200K-$1M ARR, strong retention (>120% NRR), 20-50 customers in target vertical
- **Team**: Domain expert founder(s) from the industry they're disrupting + technical co-founder
- **Market**: Specific vertical (healthcare ops, construction, logistics, insurance, agriculture) where incumbents are legacy and AI can 10x workflows
- **Product**: Deep vertical integration — not a horizontal AI tool applied to a vertical, but purpose-built for the domain
- **Why we invest**: Deep moat from vertical expertise, high switching costs, strong unit economics

## Disqualifiers
- No technical co-founder
- Burning >$200K/month pre-revenue with no clear path to efficiency
- Market too small (<$1B TAM)
- Competitive landscape already crowded with well-funded incumbents
- Founder unable to articulate why AI is necessary (vs. traditional software)

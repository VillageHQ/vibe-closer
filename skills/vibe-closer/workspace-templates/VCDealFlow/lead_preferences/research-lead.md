# Research Lead

Before reaching out to a founder, complete these research steps:

## 1. Company Research
- Visit company website via {{WEBSITE_CRAWLING}}
- Understand: what they build, who they sell to, pricing model
- Look for: traction metrics, customer logos, recent announcements
- Check: tech blog, open-source repos, product changelog

## 2. Founder Research
- Check founder profile via {{PROFILE_ENRICHMENT}}
- Background: previous companies, roles, education
- Domain expertise: did they live the problem they're solving?
- Thought leadership: blog posts, tweets, conference talks
- Previous fundraising: angels, accelerators, prior rounds

## 3. Market & Competitive Landscape
- Who are the direct competitors? How funded are they?
- What's the market timing — why now?
- Are incumbents asleep or actively innovating?
- Recent exits or IPOs in the space?

## 4. Thesis Fit Assessment
- Does AI need to be the core architecture for this to work?
- Is the TAM large enough ($1B+)?
- Can this be a fund-returner at our ownership target?
- Would our portfolio network and LP base add real value?

## 5. Warm Path Check
- Query {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} for mutual connections
- Check if portfolio founders know them or have opinions
- Look for co-investor overlap (did a trusted firm lead their pre-seed?)

## Output Format
After research, summarize:
- **Company**: {name} — {one-liner}
- **Founders**: {names, backgrounds}
- **Traction**: {ARR, customers, growth rate}
- **Thesis fit**: {strong/medium/weak} — {why}
- **Warm paths**: {list connections or "none found"}
- **Key question**: {the one thing you need to understand in the first meeting}

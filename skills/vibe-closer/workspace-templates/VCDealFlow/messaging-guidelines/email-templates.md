# Email Templates

## Ask for Startup Intro

**Used for**: Asking a mutual connection to introduce you to a founder or startup you're interested in.

**Subject**: intro to {founder_first_name} at {company_name}?

**Body**:
```
Hi {connection_name},

Hope you're doing well! I noticed you're connected with {founder_first_name} {founder_last_name} at {company_name}. We've been tracking {relevant industry or space} and {company_name} caught our eye — {why the company is interesting — mention specific product, traction, or market signal}.

Would you be comfortable making a quick intro? Happy to draft something if helpful.

Thanks!
{signature}
```

---

## Startup Intro Follow-Up

**Used for**: Nudging the connector when they haven't responded to your intro request.

**Subject**: Re: intro to {founder_first_name} at {company_name}?

**Body**:
```
Hey {connection_name},

Just bumping this up in case it got buried. Would really appreciate your help connecting with {founder_first_name} if you're comfortable.

No worries if the timing isn't right!

{signature}
```

## Direct Founder Email

**Used for**: Cold outreach to a founder when no warm path exists. Only for strong thesis fits.

**Subject**: {company_name} — would love to chat

**Body**:
```
Hi {founder_first_name},

I've been following {company_name} and {unique fit — mention something specific about their product, traction, market, or recent milestone that aligns with your investment thesis}.

We're actively investing in this space and would love to learn more about what you're building. Open to a quick chat?

{signature}
```

## Cold Email with Warm Lever

**Used for**: Direct outreach to a founder when you have a mutual connection — email the founder directly, mention the connection as social proof.

**Subject**: {founder_first_name} — quick note

**Body**:
```
Hi {founder_first_name},

{connection_name} and I are connected and when I came across {company_name}, I thought it'd be worth reaching out.

{what specifically caught your attention — mention their product approach, traction metric, or market insight that aligns with your thesis}

We're investing in this space at Horizon and would love to learn more about what you're building.

{signature}
```

## Post-First Meeting Follow-Up

**Subject**: great conversation — {topic discussed}

**Body**:
```
Hi {founder_first_name},

Thanks for the time today. I appreciated hearing about {specific thing they shared about the product or market}.

Here's what stood out to me:
- {key insight about their product or approach}
- {question or area you want to dig deeper on}
- {what excites you about the opportunity}

Next steps from our side:
- {your action item — e.g., "I'll share this with my partners this week"}
- {their action item — e.g., "Would love to see the data room when ready"}
- {timeline — e.g., "Let's plan to reconnect next Thursday"}

Looking forward to continuing the conversation.

{signature}
```

## Pass / Not Right Now

**Used for**: Gracefully declining after evaluating a startup. Be honest and helpful.

**Subject**: thanks {founder_first_name} — appreciated the conversation

**Body**:
```
Hi {founder_first_name},

Thanks for sharing {company_name} with us. {brief genuine compliment about what impressed you}.

After discussing internally, {honest reason for passing — e.g., "we feel the timing is early for our thesis" or "the round size is outside our current parameters" or "we're less convicted on the market segment"}.

I'd love to stay in touch as you scale. {specific milestone that would make you reconsider — e.g., "If you hit $500K ARR and the enterprise motion is working, I'd love to revisit"}. Happy to {offer of help — intro to another investor, feedback on positioning, etc.}.

Rooting for you.

{signature}
```

# Tone & Voice

## Voice Definition
Curious, collaborative, and founder-friendly. You're a partner, not a gatekeeper. Show genuine interest in what they're building — ask smart questions, reference specific details about their product or market. Be direct about your interest level and process.

## Do
- Lead with what specifically caught your attention about their company
- Reference their product, traction, or market with specifics — show you've done homework
- Be transparent about your thesis, check size, and timeline
- Ask thoughtful questions that show you understand their space
- Keep emails concise — founders are busy building
- Respond quickly to warm intros (within 24 hours)
- When passing, explain why briefly and offer to be helpful (intros to other investors, feedback)

## Don't
- Use generic VC language ("excited about the space", "interesting traction")
- Send batch emails — every outreach must be individually personalized
- Name-drop portfolio companies without a genuine connection to the founder's work
- Pressure founders for meetings — make it easy to say no
- Ghost founders — always close the loop, even if it's a pass
- Oversell what you bring — be honest about fund size, support model, and decision process
- Ask for a deck before showing genuine interest in their problem

## Subject Lines
- Lowercase, specific to their company or space
- Good: "{company_name} — would love to chat", "your approach to {specific thing}"
- Bad: "Investment Opportunity Discussion", "Horizon Ventures Introduction"

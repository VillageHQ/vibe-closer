# Ideal Investor Profiles

Define the types of investors who are the best fit for your company. Tailor all outreach to investors matching these profiles.

## ICP 1: Seed-Stage AI/SaaS VCs

### Firm Profile
- **Stage**: Seed (some do Pre-seed to Seed+)
- **Check size range**: $500K - $2.5M
- **Industry focus**: Applied AI, B2B SaaS, developer tools, vertical AI
- **Geographic focus**: US-based, some invest globally
- **Fund type**: Dedicated seed funds and early-stage generalist funds with strong AI portfolios

### Thesis Alignment
- These firms have a stated thesis around applied AI transforming enterprise workflows — not foundational model research, but AI embedded into real business processes
- Portfolio companies often include AI-first B2B tools: companies like Jasper (content), Harvey (legal), Glean (search) demonstrate the "AI copilot for [function]" pattern they love
- They look for technical founders with domain expertise who've seen the problem firsthand
- Speed of revenue traction matters — they want to see product-market fit signals early

### Evaluation Criteria
- Revenue traction relative to stage: $100K+ ARR at Seed is strong signal
- Net revenue retention above 120% indicates real value delivery
- Team quality: technical depth + domain expertise is the combination they prize
- Market timing: are customers actively pulling for this solution?
- Due diligence typically takes 2-4 weeks with 2-3 partner meetings
- Most want a board seat or board observer for checks above $1M

### Key Decision Makers
- Target the partner who covers AI/ML or SaaS infrastructure investments
- Many firms have dedicated AI partners post-2023 — look for recent AI portfolio additions
- Operating partners with CX or enterprise sales backgrounds add diligence value

### Why They're a Fit
- Lumina AI's 3x MoM growth and $180K ARR at Seed stage is in top-decile for their portfolio benchmarks
- Zero churn + 135% NRR signals genuine product-market fit
- Priya's Zendesk background (built their ML routing) gives deep domain credibility
- "AI copilot for support" sits squarely in the applied AI thesis these firms champion
- $24B market with clear AI disruption timing — this is the moment they want to invest

### Potential Concerns / Mitigations
- **"What about Zendesk/Intercom building this natively?"** — Incumbents are bolting AI onto legacy architectures. Lumina is built AI-first. History shows vertical AI startups outpace incumbents (Veeva vs. Salesforce Health Cloud pattern). We also integrate with these platforms, so we're a complement today, not a competitor
- **"28 customers is still early"** — True, but zero churn and 3x MoM growth with a 5-person team shows capital efficiency. This round funds the GTM engine to scale
- **"Auto-resolution at 28% — is that enough?"** — 28% is day-one value. It compounds: our top customers hit 38% after 90 days as the model learns from corrections. Roadmap targets 45% by year end

### Example Firms
- First Round Capital, Floodgate, Craft Ventures, Haystack, Boldstart Ventures, Bowery Capital

## ICP 2: Strategic Angels — Customer Experience Operators

### Firm Profile
- **Stage**: Seed and Pre-seed (personal checks)
- **Check size range**: $25K - $150K
- **Industry focus**: Customer experience, support operations, SaaS platforms
- **Geographic focus**: Global (angels invest regardless of geography)
- **Fund type**: Personal capital, sometimes through AngelList syndicates

### Thesis Alignment
- These are former executives at Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk, Salesforce Service Cloud, Twilio, or high-growth companies where they led support/CX functions
- They've lived the pain of scaling support teams — they understand the problem viscerally
- They invest in categories they know deeply, and their conviction comes from recognizing the product solves a real problem they personally experienced
- They value founders who "get it" — Priya's Zendesk background creates immediate trust

### Evaluation Criteria
- Product quality: they'll actually try the product or watch a demo and evaluate it as an operator
- Founder-market fit: does this team understand the nuances of support workflows?
- Customer love: do support teams genuinely prefer using the product?
- Less focused on traditional VC metrics; more on "would I have bought this when I was running support at [company]?"

### Key Decision Makers
- Individual decision — no IC or partners to convince
- Often decide within 1-2 meetings
- Referrals from other operators carry enormous weight

### Why They're a Fit
- Lumina solves a problem they know intimately — they're pre-sold on the category
- Their names on the cap table signal domain credibility to institutional investors
- They provide invaluable product feedback, customer intros, and hiring referrals
- Priya's Zendesk network makes these warm intros natural

### Potential Concerns / Mitigations
- **"Is AI good enough yet for complex support?"** — Demo the product. Show multi-turn ticket resolution with citations. Let the product speak
- **"Why not just use ChatGPT/Claude directly?"** — Generic LLMs don't handle support-specific workflows: escalation logic, ticket routing, tone matching, knowledge base integration, agent feedback loops. Show the difference side-by-side

### Where to Find Them
- Priya's direct Zendesk network (40+ support leaders)
- LinkedIn search: former VP/Director of Support at high-growth SaaS companies
- Support Driven community, CX industry Slack groups
- Conference speakers at Zendesk Relate, Support Driven Expo, CX North America

## ICP 3: Institutional Seed Funds with AI Thesis

### Firm Profile
- **Stage**: Seed (occasionally Pre-seed)
- **Check size range**: $250K - $1.5M
- **Industry focus**: Generalist or broad tech focus, but with a recently stated AI thesis or partner who covers AI
- **Geographic focus**: US, some global
- **Fund type**: Institutional seed funds, emerging managers (Fund I-III)

### Thesis Alignment
- These are broader seed funds that may not be "AI-first" but have recognized AI as a major investment theme and allocated a portion of their portfolio to it
- They often have a partner who's building conviction in AI and looking for their defining investment in the space
- They value large market + clear wedge: "AI copilot for support" in a $24B market is a clean narrative
- They often co-invest alongside a lead, so they're great for filling out the round

### Evaluation Criteria
- Market size and growth trajectory matter more than current revenue
- Team pedigree: MIT/Stanford credentials + Zendesk/McKinsey experience resonate
- Competitive moat: what prevents big platforms from replicating this?
- Unit economics: even at seed, they want to see a path to efficient growth
- Due diligence is typically 3-6 weeks, sometimes longer for first AI investment

### Key Decision Makers
- Partner with the AI or SaaS coverage area
- Sometimes a more junior partner or principal building their AI portfolio — these can be strong champions

### Why They're a Fit
- Lumina is a clean, understandable AI investment: clear problem, clear buyer, clear metrics
- Lower risk than "moonshot AI" — this is applied AI with paying customers today
- Strong co-investment opportunity alongside a lead VC
- The CX market is familiar to generalist investors (most have Zendesk/Freshdesk in their portfolio or know the space)

### Potential Concerns / Mitigations
- **"We don't have deep AI expertise to evaluate the tech"** — Offer to connect them with our technical advisors (ex-Anthropic engineer, Freshdesk CTO) who can speak to the architecture
- **"Is the AI moat real?"** — Our moat isn't the model — it's the data flywheel. Every customer's agent corrections improve the system. After 90 days, each deployment is deeply customized to that company's products, tone, and workflows. Switching costs are high because the value compounds
- **"We want a lead, not a follow"** — Position as co-lead opportunity at $500K-$1M if they want to step up

### Example Firms
- Precursor Ventures, Afore Capital, Chapter One, Hustle Fund, Banana Capital, Contrary

## Portfolio Conflicts

Avoid investors with existing portfolio companies in direct competition:
- **Forethought** (AI for support — raised Series C): check cap tables of investors like NEA, Autotech
- **Thankful** (AI agent for CX — acquired by Gladly): investors may still have conflict
- **Cognigy** (conversational AI platform with CX focus): Insight Partners portfolio
- **Ada** (AI customer service automation): Spark Capital, Accel portfolio
- **Ultimate.ai** (acquired by Zendesk): previous investors may have non-compete or right of first refusal

Before reaching out to any firm, check their portfolio page for AI support/CX investments. If uncertain, ask during the first meeting: "Do you have any portfolio companies in the AI-powered customer support space?"

# Workflow Planner

## Outreach Sequencing Rules

1. **Always prioritize warm introductions first**
   - Fetch paths using {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}}
   - Look for mutual connections who can introduce you
   - Have the mutual connection send a forwardable blurb email
   - Even a weak warm path (friend-of-friend) is better than a cold email — always exhaust warm options first

2. **If no direct warm path exists, look for indirect connections**
   - Check if anyone in your network knows the investor's portfolio founders
   - Portfolio founders introducing you to their own investors is a strong signal
   - Ask advisors, board members, and existing investors for their networks
   - Check if the investor has spoken at events where you have connections
   - Include your forwardable blurb so they can easily forward

3. **Only cold email as an absolute last resort**
   - Cold outreach to investors has a <5% response rate — invest time in finding warm paths instead
   - If you must go cold, write a highly personalized email mentioning a specific portfolio company or thesis
   - Reference a recent announcement, event, podcast appearance, or article by the investor
   - Cold email should be reserved for investors where: (a) no warm path exists after thorough search, AND (b) thesis fit is exceptionally strong
   - Never batch-send cold emails — each one must be individually crafted

4. **Follow-up sequencing**
   - If no reply after 3 business days, send a follow-up email (in recipient's timezone)
   - After 1 week with no reply, send a LinkedIn message if possible
   - Max 2 follow-ups before moving to "no reply" status
   - Always draft follow-ups for review before sending
   - Add new traction data or a relevant insight in each follow-up — never just "bumping this"

5. **Data room and documentation**
   - Once investor expresses interest, send data room access link
   - Include a cover email highlighting sections most relevant to their thesis
   - Follow up after 1 week to see if they have questions

6. **Managing competitive dynamics**
   - When you have 2+ firms in partner meetings, communicate timeline transparently
   - Let each firm know others are in process without naming names
   - Set a decision deadline that gives firms enough time but creates urgency
   - Never bluff about competing term sheets — it destroys trust

## Pipeline Stages

1. **New Lead** — Investor identified and qualified
2. **Intro Requested** — Asked for warm intro; awaiting mutual connection response
3. **Contacted** — Initial outreach sent (warm intro, personalized email, or LinkedIn message)
4. **Replied** — Investor replied to initial outreach
5. **First Meeting Scheduled** — Calendar invite sent and accepted
6. **First Meeting Complete** — Met with investor; discussing further
7. **Partner Meeting** — Passed to investment committee or additional partners
8. **Due Diligence** — Investor reviewing company materials, data room, references
9. **Term Sheet** — Investor has sent term sheet for review
10. **Closed Won** — Investment completed and funds received
11. **Closed Lost** — Investor declined or conversation ended

## Key Principles

- **Warm always beats cold**: Spend energy finding mutual connections before cold outreach. A warm intro from a trusted source converts at 10-20x the rate of a cold email
- **Forwardable blurbs are essential**: Make it easy for mutual connections to introduce you. They shouldn't have to write anything — just forward your blurb
- **Investor profile matters**: Tailor all messaging to their stated thesis, portfolio, and stage preference
- **Data room is your demo**: Once they're interested, share organized, investor-friendly materials
- **Follow up respectfully**: Give time between touches, never spam
- **Batch your process**: Run a tight process — aim to have 8-10 meetings per week during peak outreach so multiple firms are moving through the pipeline simultaneously
- **Capture meeting notes immediately**: After every investor meeting, record key questions, concerns, next steps, and follow-up items. These notes drive your follow-up strategy

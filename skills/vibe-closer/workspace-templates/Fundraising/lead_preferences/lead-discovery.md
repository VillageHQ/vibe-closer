# Lead Discovery

Investor discovery is about finding high-quality targets who are a fit for your company, then mapping warm paths to them.

## Investor Sources

### 1. **Warm Relationship Mapping** — Start Here
   - Use {{FETCH_RELATIONSHIPS}} to identify warm paths to target investors
   - Map your network: who knows investors at your target firms?
   - Ask for warm introductions before cold outreach
   - This is your most effective channel — prioritize it

### 2. **AngelList / Syndicates**
   - Search for investors with your keywords (e.g., "fintech", "healthcare AI")
   - Filter by: stage they invest in, check size, location
   - Look for angels active on your platform who've invested in similar companies
   - **Advantage**: Often more accessible than VCs; faster decisions
   - **Source these from**: AngelList, Swoop, Republic, AngelHub

### 3. **Crunchbase Signals**
   - Search VC firms by focus: industry, stage, check size
   - Look for recent fund closes (newly raised capital = actively investing)
   - Check their portfolio for similar companies
   - Export lists of target firms and individual partners
   - **Tip**: Filter for firms that have announced new funds in the last 6-12 months

### 4. **LinkedIn Search**
   - Search for "Partner" + "[sector]" at target firms
   - Look at portfolio companies and see who led those investments
   - Follow active investors and monitor their posts for thesis clues
   - Check who's writing about trends relevant to your space
   - **Tip**: VCs who post frequently are usually more accessible

### 5. **Conference and Event Attendees**
   - Pull attendee lists from events in your space (TechCrunch Disrupt, etc.)
   - Research which VCs attended
   - Start with the VCs who showed up (= they're interested in your space)

### 6. **Pitch Databases / Operator Platforms**
   - Operators.vc, Forge, On Deck — communities of founders and investors
   - Join relevant cohorts and get introductions through cohort mates
   - These often surface warm paths naturally

### 7. **Portfolio Backtracking**
   - Find 3-5 companies similar to yours that raised recently
   - Look at who led their rounds
   - If they invested in a competitor or adjacent company, they're likely interested in your space

### 8. **Fund GP / LP Lists**
   - Use Preqin, PitchBook, or CB Insights to find fund information
   - Look at fund descriptions and stated thesis
   - Identify GPs (partners) who make the decisions
   - Check LP list to see if any LPs are also operating angel investors

## Qualification Criteria

A potential investor qualifies if:
- **Stage match**: Invests at your stage (seed, Series A, etc.)
- **Check size match**: Their typical check size matches your needs
- **Thesis alignment**: Their stated focus overlaps with your market/problem
- **No portfolio conflicts**: Doesn't have conflicting portfolio companies
- **Decision-maker identified**: You know who specifically leads investments
- **Contact method available**: Email or LinkedIn is accessible

## Research Template for Each Investor

When you identify a prospect, research:

1. **Firm basics**
   - Fund size and stage focus
   - Geographic focus
   - Sector focus
   - Recent fund close date

2. **Their thesis**
   - What they say publicly (website, recent interviews)
   - What their portfolio reveals (what do they actually invest in?)
   - Recent announcements or posts from partners
   - Any misalignment with your company?

3. **Decision makers**
   - Who leads deals in your space / stage?
   - Partner names, backgrounds, specialties
   - Operating partners who add value?

4. **Your fit**
   - Why would they want to invest in YOU specifically?
   - Any portfolio companies similar to yours?
   - Do you share advisor networks or experiences?
   - What would be your strong opening angle with them?

5. **Warm paths**
   - Who do you know that knows them?
   - Who in your network knows their portfolio companies?
   - Any mutual advisors or board members?

## Prioritization

**Rank investors by:**
1. **Warm path strength** (Do you have a strong mutual connection?)
2. **Thesis fit** (How aligned is their thesis with you?)
3. **Check size appropriateness** (Can they write a check your size?)
4. **Speed** (Do they have a history of fast decisions?)

## Output Format

For each discovered investor, provide:
- **Investor / Firm name**: [Name] at [Firm]
- **Stage and check size**: [e.g., Seed, $250K-$1M]
- **Thesis fit**: [Why they matter to you]
- **Warm path**: [Mutual connection name, if available]
- **Primary contact**: [Partner name and email/LinkedIn]
- **Next action**: [Warm intro request, cold email, LinkedIn message]

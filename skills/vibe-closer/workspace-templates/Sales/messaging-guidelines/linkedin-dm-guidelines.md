# LinkedIn DM Guidelines

## Structure
- Max 80 words (LinkedIn messages should be noticeably shorter than email)
- No subject line needed
- Conversational tone — this is a chat, not a formal email
- One idea per message. Don't cram your pitch + CTA + social proof into a single DM.

## When to Use LinkedIn
- As a secondary channel after email gets no reply (per workflow planner: send LinkedIn message alongside email follow-up at day 3)
- When you have no email address for the prospect
- When the prospect is clearly active on LinkedIn (posts within the last 2 weeks)
- When you want to reference a specific LinkedIn post or comment they made

## Timing
- Send during business hours in the prospect's timezone
- Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am or 12-1pm local time
- Avoid weekends and Monday mornings

## Do
- Reference a specific post, comment, or accomplishment of theirs
- Keep the first message to 2-3 sentences maximum
- Ask a genuine question related to their work (not a disguised pitch)
- Wait for a reply before pitching anything — the first message is about starting a conversation
- Use their tone — if they post casually, write casually
- Mention Metricflow's relevance to something they've publicly shared

## Don't
- Copy-paste the email you already sent them
- Send a connection request + pitch in the same message
- Use LinkedIn for long-form messages (that's what email is for)
- Send a pitch immediately after they accept your connection request — wait at least a few hours
- Use "I'd love to pick your brain" or "Can I get 15 minutes?"
- Send voice notes or video messages unsolicited

## Connection Request Note (max 300 characters)
```
Hi {{first_name}} — I work on analytics infrastructure at Metricflow. [1 sentence referencing their work: e.g., "Liked your post about observability tradeoffs" or "Saw your team is scaling the data platform"]. Would love to connect.
```

## First DM After Connection (send a few hours after they accept)
```
Hey {{first_name}} — thanks for connecting.

[Reference something specific: their recent post, a talk, their company's growth, a job posting on their team.]

Curious — how is your team handling metric consistency across [tools/services]? It's a problem we keep hearing about from [their industry] engineering teams.
```

## Follow-Up DM (if no reply after 5 business days)
```
No worries if the timing's off, {{first_name}}. Just came across [specific resource: a case study, benchmark, or article relevant to their situation] and thought it might be useful for what you're building at {{company}}.

[Link to resource]
```

## Engaging With Their Content (before or alongside outreach)
- Like and comment on their posts with substantive observations (not just "Great post!")
- Share their content with a thoughtful addition if relevant to Metricflow's space
- This builds familiarity before your DM arrives — they're more likely to respond to someone they've "seen around"

## Example Good DM
> Hey Marcus — your post about consolidating from 4 monitoring tools to 2 hit close to home. We're building the metrics layer that makes that consolidation actually work (consistent definitions across whatever tools you keep). Curious if that's still an active project for your team?

## Example Bad DM
> Hi Marcus! I'm with Metricflow and we have an amazing analytics platform that helps engineering teams. I'd love to schedule a quick call to show you how we can transform your data infrastructure. Are you free this week?
